Vegetable Biryani

Ingredients

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrot, beans, peas, potatoes)
  • 1 large onion, thinly sliced
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp ghee or oil
  • 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 2 green chilies, slit
  • 1/4 cup fresh coriander and mint leaves, chopped
  • Whole spices: 4 cloves, 4 cardamoms, 1-inch cinnamon, 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 3 cups water

Nutrition Facts (approximate per serving)

Calories: 350 kcal

Fat: 10g

Carbohydrates: 55g

Protein: 7g

Fiber: 5g

Sodium: 400mg

Cooking Method

Step 1Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes. Drain and set aside.
Step 2Heat ghee/oil in a heavy-bottomed pot. Add whole spices and sauté till aromatic.
Step 3Add sliced onions and sauté till golden brown.
Step 4Add ginger-garlic paste and green chilies; cook for 2 minutes.
Step 5Add chopped tomatoes, turmeric, red chili powder, and salt. Cook till tomatoes soften.
Step 6Mix in the yogurt and cook till oil separates from the masala.
Step 7Add mixed vegetables and cook for 5-7 minutes.
Step 8Add soaked rice and gently mix to coat rice with masala.
Step 9Add water and bring to a boil. Reduce heat, cover, and cook until rice is tender.
Step 10Sprinkle garam masala, chopped coriander, and mint leaves. Mix gently.
Step 11Turn off heat and let the biryani rest for 10 minutes before serving.

Frequently Asked Questions

Yogurt adds tanginess and helps tenderize vegetables, but you can skip it if preferred.

Use fresh spices and herbs, and allow the biryani to rest before serving for flavors to blend.
